## 🚀 Some projects

### [Undergraduate Thesis (TCC): A Study of the Mathematical Epidemiology of Tuberculosis in Brazil](https://github.com/Leprechas/TCC_Tuberculose/tree/main)
Undergraduate thesis spanning mathematical epidemiology, social epidemiology, history, and biology at Ilum – School of Science – CNPEM.

### [Mathematical modeling of dengue transmission in the state of São Paulo: coupled SIR–ASI models with climate change scenarios](https://github.com/Leprechas/Estudo-de-transmiss-o-da-dengue-Aplica-o-no-estado-de-S-o-Paulo-considerando-as-mudan-as-clim-ticas)
In this work, we developed a mathematical framework that couples two compartmental models: an SIR (Susceptible–Infected–Recovered) model with vital dynamics for the human population, and an ASI (Aquatic–Susceptible–Infected) model for the population dynamics of the *Aedes aegypti* vector, incorporating transovarial transmission and a seasonal factor for standing-water availability.

### [EpiBrasil-painel](https://github.com/Leprechas/epibrasil-painel)
EpiBrasil is a static epidemiological dashboard for the exploratory visualization of notifiable diseases and health conditions in Brazil, with data aggregated by municipality, federative unit (state), year, and disease.

### [A study of the population dynamics of bacterial growth in the laboratory using ordinary and interval differential equations](https://github.com/Leprechas/Crescimento_Bacteriano)
This work studied the population dynamics of *Escherichia coli* growth in the laboratory using Ordinary Differential Equations (ODEs) and Interval Differential Equations (IDEs) from the Malthus and Verhulst models, solved through analytical methods and the numerical methods of Euler and fourth-order Runge–Kutta, with the goal of evaluating the best strategy to model this population.
